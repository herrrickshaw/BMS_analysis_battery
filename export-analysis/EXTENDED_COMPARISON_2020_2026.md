## Part 3: 2025-26 Projections

### Conservative Forecast Methodology

**Approach**: Use verified 2020-2023 CAGR, apply conservative caps to extreme growth rates

#### Projection Rules Applied

1. **Sustainable commodities** (CAGR <100%): Project using historical CAGR
2. **High-growth commodities** (CAGR 100-300%): Cap at 50% CAGR forward
3. **Extreme growth** (CAGR >300%): Cap at 30% CAGR forward
4. **Emerging commodities** (zero baseline): Project from 2023 base +20% annually

**Rationale**: Extreme growth rates are typically recovery-driven (post-COVID) and unsustainable.

## Conclusion

**Verified Growth 2020-2023**: India's exports grew 44.1% ($623B → $898B)

**Conservative 2026 Projection**: $1.08-1.30T total exports
- Additional $180-400B growth from 2023 baseline
- Driven primarily by sustainable sectors (vehicles, pharma, chemicals)
- Recovery-phase commodities (aviation, aircraft) may moderate

**Strategic Recommendation**:
- **Strong sectors** (vehicles, pharma, chemicals): Expect 15-30% compound annual growth through 2026
- **Emerging sectors** (specialty fabrics, new compounds): Monitor for scaling potential
- **Mature sectors** (diamonds, basic commodities): Plan for flat-to-declining growth

**Investment Thesis**: India's export sector continues strong growth trajectory with diversification into high-value sectors, positioned for $1.1-1.3T total value by 2026.
