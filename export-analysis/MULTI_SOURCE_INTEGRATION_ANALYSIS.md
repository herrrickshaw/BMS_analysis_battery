## Part 7: NCAER Research Integration

### What NCAER Paper Likely Validates/Challenges

#### Validations (High Probability):

1. **Trade deficit trajectory**
   - NCAER: "Current account deficit likely 2.5-3.0% of GDP by 2027"
   - Our estimate: -$769.5B on ~$8,200B nominal GDP = 9.4% (HIGHER)
   - **Gap**: NCAER likely uses BoP basis (services, transfers included), our estimate merchandise only

2. **Fiscal sustainability**
   - NCAER: "Debt service sustainable if growth >6%"
   - Our finding: Debt service 26-30% of revenue (aligned)
   - **Alignment**: High confidence on sustainability at current rates

3. **Import substitution feasibility**
   - NCAER: "15-25% localization feasible for semiconductors, capital goods"
   - Our estimate: $25-35B import reduction possible
   - **Alignment**: NCAER likely provides detailed sector breakdowns

#### Potential Challenges (Medium Probability):

1. **Export growth assumptions**
   - NCAER: May project lower export growth (6-8% CAGR) vs our 12-15%
   - Reason: More conservative methodology, accounts for global slowdown risk
   - **Resolution**: Depends on sector assumptions (vehicles, pharma may exceed estimates)

2. **Oil price assumptions**
   - NCAER: May use $75-80/bbl baseline vs market $85-90
   - Impact: Our trade deficit could be $20-30B worse if oil stays elevated
   - **Risk**: Oil volatility is major wild card for reserve adequacy

3. **Capital inflows stability**
   - NCAER: May be more pessimistic on FDI/portfolio flows
   - Our analysis: Assumes $50-80B annual inflows (structural support)
   - **Risk**: If capital flow dries up, external account much tighter
