```
├─ Pandemic spending obscured by low base
├─ Status: MANAGEABLE

├─ First pandemic bonds maturing
├─ Status: BEGINNING TO RISE

FY 2022-23: ~₹28-30 lakh Cr (20% of borrowing)
├─ More pandemic bonds maturing
├─ Status: RISING

FY 2023-24: ~₹33-36 lakh Cr (22% of borrowing)
├─ Accelerating maturity wall
├─ Status: NOTICEABLY HIGHER

FY 2025-26: ₹37-40 lakh Cr (22-25% of borrowing, estimated)
├─ Maturity wall building
├─ Status: SIGNIFICANT

FY 2026-27: ₹54.68 lakh Cr (32% of borrowing!) - WORST
├─ 2026-27 maturity peak
├─ Bonds issued 2020-2023 all maturing
├─ Status: CRISIS LEVEL
```

### Refinancing Burden Deterioration

```
BEST YEAR:              FY 2019-20: 14% of borrowing
CURRENT:                FY 2025-26: 22-25% of borrowing
WORST YEAR:             FY 2026-27: 32% of borrowing (PROJECTED)

Deterioration pattern:
- FY 2019-20 to 2026-27: +18 percentage points DOUBLING
- This means: 32% of new borrowing goes to repay old debt
- Only 68% available for new government spending

Annual increase rate:
- Average: +2-3 pp per year
- Not slowing; accelerating into 2026-27

KEY INSIGHT:
This is the TICKING TIME BOMB.
In 2026-27, government must borrow ₹1.72 lakh Cr.
But ₹0.55 lakh Cr (32%) goes to repay matured bonds.
Only ₹1.17 lakh Cr (68%) available for new spending/capex.

This explains why import-substitution capex MUST happen in 2026-27:
- This is the LAST year before maturity wall gets worse
- If capex delayed to 2027-28, refinancing burden falls naturally
- But then no import substitution capex, trade deficit doesn't improve
- Catch-22: Execute capex now at peak refinancing, or delay and miss window

CRITICAL TIMING DECISION POINT.
```

### Tax Collection Deterioration Pattern

```
BEST COLLECTION RATE:   FY 2022-23: 6.0% (peak efficiency)
CURRENT:                FY 2025-26: 6.0% (no improvement)
TREND:                  FLAT, NOT IMPROVING

The Problem:
- Despite 7-8% nominal GDP growth
- Despite 12% tax revenue growth (recovery phase)
- Tax/GDP ratio hasn't budged above 6%
- This suggests: Tax base NOT expanding with economy
- Root cause: Informal economy, incomplete GST base, tax evasion

Implication:
- Government cannot grow revenue faster than GDP
- This is a STRUCTURAL CONSTRAINT
- Cannot be solved by "economic growth" alone
- Requires NEW tax measures (GST rate increases, base broadening, compliance)

- Revenue growing at 8% (budget projection 2026-27)
- But GDP growing at 7% (RBI estimate)
- So ratio improves by mere 0.1 pp (6.0% → 6.1%)
- This is INSUFFICIENT to ease fiscal pressure

For comparison:
- OECD average tax/GDP: 20-25%
- India's tax/GDP: 6%
- India is collecting 1/3 to 1/4 of developed country rate
- But developing nation spending needs similar to developed countries
- This is the MISMATCH creating fiscal stress
```
