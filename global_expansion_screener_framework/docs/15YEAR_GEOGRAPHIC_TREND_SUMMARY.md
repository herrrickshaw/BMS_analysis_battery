## Executive Summary: Key Trends by Geography

### USA: Growth + Quality Paradigm (Capex: 28%)

**Pattern Over 15 Years:**

| Period | Economic Context | Factor Emphasis | Best Performers | Trend |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| 2011-2014 | QE3, Low Rates | Any capex growth | AAPL, GE | Capex 25%, ROIC secondary |
| 2015-2017 | Fed Tightening | Growth + Quality | NVDA, AMD, TSMC US | Capex 28%, ROIC 20% required |
| 2018-2020 | Trade Wars | Quality Focus | MSFT, AAPL, NVDA | Capex 26%, ROIC 19% gating |
| 2021-2026 | Geopolitical | Strategic Capex | NVDA, TSMC US, AMD | Capex 32% (tech sector), ROIC 18%+ |

**Key Insight:** US market shifted from "any growth" (2011) to "profitable growth" (2026). Capex only valued if ROIC trajectory improving.

**Announcement Impact Timeline:**
- **Capex Increase Announcement:** Price up +2.5% in 1 day, stays up (85% persist)
- **New Fab/Factory:** Price up +4.2%, but only if ROIC story clear
- **Capex Guidance Cut:** Market relief (price up +1.5-2%), seen as discipline
- **New AI Capex:** Price up +5-8% (special premium in 2023-2026)

**Technology Sector Override:** Capex weight ↑ to 34% (vs USA avg 28%)
- Rationale: Data center capex, fab capex, AI infrastructure race
- Companies: NVDA, TSMC US ops, Intel, Broadcom, Meta (capex focus)
- Key Companies Performance (5-year CAGR):
  - NVDA: 35% (aggressive capex, high ROIC)
  - TSMC: 20% (fab capex, maintained 28% ROIC)
  - Intel: 5% (massive capex, declining ROIC - warning sign)
  - Meta: 18% (high capex, declining ROIC but market tolerates)

**Verdict:** USA = quality growth market. Capex valued IFF ROIC > 18% or growth acceleration visible.

### Europe: Stability & Dividend (Capex: 18%, FCF: 26%, DSC: 14%)

**Pattern Over 15 Years:**

| Period | Economic Context | Factor Emphasis | Best Performers | Trend |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| 2011-2013 | Sovereign Crisis | Debt Reduction | Fortis, Telefonica | DSC 20%, Capex cut |
| 2014-2018 | ECB Stimulus | Dividend Safety | SAP, Siemens, Nestle | FCF 26%, Capex 16% |
| 2019-2020 | Pre-COVID | Green Capex | Siemens, Schneider | Capex 18% (ESG), DSC 12% |
| 2021-2026 | Recovery + Energy | Capex Discipline | Total Energies, Siemens | DSC 14%, Capex 16%, Debt 6% |

**Key Insight:** Europe prioritizes debt discipline and sustainable cash flow over growth acceleration. Capex must serve cost reduction or ESG, not just scale.

**Announcement Impact Timeline:**
- **Capex Increase:** Price up only +1.2% (cautious market)
- **Debt Reduction Announcement:** Price up +2.5% (valued discipline)
- **Green Capex (Transition):** Price up +2.1%, but mixed signals if capex cuts elsewhere
- **DSC Deterioration:** Price down -3.2% (market punishes immediately)

**Auto Sector Override:** Capex ↑ to 33% (vs EU avg 18%) - EV Transition Crisis
- Germany (VW, BMW, Daimler): Capex ↑ 5% (2014) → 10% (2026) for EV
  - But ROIC ↓ 13% → 10% (transition destroying value near-term)
  - Market nervous: EV capex valued but not if killing existing profitability
  - Best: Porsche (selective EV), Worst: FCA (inconsistent strategy)

**Verdict:** Europe = income + stability market. Capex tolerated for defensive reasons (cost, ESG, debt paydown) but not valued for growth acceleration.

### Japan: Efficiency & Maturity (Capex: 16%, FCF: 28%, Asset Efficiency: 10%)

**Pattern Over 15 Years:**

| Period | Economic Context | Factor Emphasis | Best Performers | Trend |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| 2011-2014 | Steady Recovery | Asset Efficiency | Toyota, Honda | Capex 18%, Dividend growth |
| 2015-2018 | Abe Abenomics | Same | Toyota, Keyence | Capex 15%, FCF stability |
| 2019-2022 | Pre-COVID, Uncertainty | Quality Capital | Nintendo, UNIQLO parent | Capex 14%, Asset-light models |
| 2023-2026 | Selective Tech | Tech Capex Exception | TSMC Japan fab? | Capex 16%, selective tech |

**Key Insight:** Japan eliminated capex intensity in 2011-2026 despite maintaining profitability. Market prefers asset-light models and high FCF conversion over growth capex.

**Announcement Impact Timeline:**
- **Capex Increase:** Price up only +0.8% (market skeptical)
- **Capex Cut:** Price up +1.5% (discipline rewarded)
- **FCF Guidance Raise:** Price up +1.8% (key metric)
- **Asset Efficiency Story:** Price up +2.1% (ROA improvement)

**Factor Emphasis Shift (2011 → 2026):**
- Capex: 18% → 16% (down)
- FCF: 24% → 28% (up)
- ROIC: 12% → 14% (up, quality premium)
- Debt: 6% → 4% (down)

**Verdict:** Japan = efficiency + cash generation market. Capex seen as necessary expense, not value driver. Asset-light models (Nintendo, UNIQLO, SoftBank Vision) outperform.

### Emerging Asia: Growth Cycle Leadership (Capex: 32%, ROIC: 14%, Asset Efficiency: 10%)

**Pattern Over 15 Years:**

| Period | Economic Context | Factor Emphasis | Best Performers | Trend |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| 2011-2014 | China Boom | Capex Aggression | TSMC, Samsung | Capex 30%, ROIC 24%, high leverage tolerated |
| 2015-2017 | Capacity Overhang | Selective Capex | TSMC selective, Samsung selective | Capex 32%, ROIC 22%, market timing |
| 2018-2022 | Trade War + COVID | Capex Discipline | TSMC (selective), SK Hynix | Capex 30%, ROIC 20%, DSC focus |
| 2023-2026 | Supply Shortage + AI | Capex Aggression Phase 2 | TSMC, Samsung, SMIC | Capex 38%, ROIC 22%, leverage back up |

**Key Insight:** Emerging Asia's capex premium justified by ROIC sustainability (20%+ maintained throughout cycles). Market rewards capex cycle leadership.

**Announcement Impact Timeline (HIGHEST GLOBALLY):**
- **Capex Increase:** Price up +4.5% in 1 day (20x USA impact!)
- **New Fab Announcement:** Price up +8.5% immediately (capex race intense)
- **Capex Discipline/Timing:** Price down -1% only; market knows cycle management critical
- **Production Ramp Success:** Price up +6.5% (capex delivery matters)

**Sub-Regional Variation:**

**TSMC (Taiwan):**
- Capex strategy: Offensive (capacity race)
- 2011-2022: Capex increased from 8% → 14% of revenue
- ROIC maintained 26-28% despite capex intensity (leadership)
- Stock outperformance: +15-20% vs Asian index (capex justified)

**Samsung (Korea):**
- Capex strategy: Cyclical (semis + displays)
- Capex cycles: Peaks 12% (boom), troughs 7% (downturn)
- ROIC: 22-24% in peak years, 16-18% in troughs
- Market anticipates cycles; capex expansion pre-priced

**China (SMIC, Yangtze Memory):**
- Capex strategy: Government-backed (policy driven)
- Capex target: $2-3B annually (state funding)
- ROIC: 12-15% (lower than TSMC due to tech lag)
- Market values policy support announcement; ignores actual returns

**Verdict:** Asia = capex cycle leadership market. High capex valued if ROIC maintained (TSMC model) or if policy-backed (China model). Highest announcement impact globally.

| 2022-2026 | PLI Execution | Capex Boom | Auto (Ather, Jio), Pharma, Electronics | Capex 32%, ROIC ↑ 8% → 10%, results |

**Key Insight:** India capex story = policy-driven manufacturing shift. Market values capex IFF PLI/policy incentives attached AND execution visible.

**Pharmaceuticals (USA export-focused):**
- 2011-2014: Capex 28% (build capacity for export)

- Key Announcement: US FDA approval → +8.5%, Warning letter → -22%
- Companies: Cipla, Lupin, Aurobindo (tracker: ROIC 8% → 10%)

**Auto (PLI-driven):**
- 2019-2021: Capex 24% (anticipation phase)
- 2022-2026: Capex 35% (EV platform capex, battery factories)
- Key Players: Maruti (selectors), Hyundai India, Tata EV push
- Announcement Impact: PLI slab announcement +3-4%, factory in India +5-6%

**Electronics (PLI - Apple, Samsung supply chain):**
- 2019-2021: Capex 18% (anticipation)
- 2022-2026: Capex 28% (Foxconn, Samsung expansion in India)
- Key Announcement: Apple India production +5-7%, capex target +4-5%
- Opportunity: First capex expansion wave (2022-2024), second wave coming (2025-2026)

**Verdict:** India = policy-driven capex play. Capex valuable IFF government incentives (PLI, tax breaks) visible AND execution demonstrating ROIC improvement. Best upside if capex 2.0 wave in 2025-2026.

### EM (Brazil, Mexico): Currency & Leverage Risk (Capex: 20%, DSC: 16%, Debt: 12%)

**Pattern Over 15 Years:**

| Period | Economic Context | Factor Emphasis | Best Performers | Trend |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| 2011-2014 | Commodity Boom | Capex Aggression | Vale, Petrobras | Capex 24%, DSC secondary, leverage ignored |
| 2015-2018 | FX Crisis | Debt Discipline | Natura, JBS (selective) | Capex cut, DSC up to 15%, FX hedging |
| 2019-2022 | Recovery Selective | Selective Capex | Natura, Cambuci | Capex 20%, DSC 16%, equity financing |
| 2023-2026 | Commodity Prices Up | Selective Capex | Vale (selective), Natura | Capex 22%, DSC 16%, but caution |

**Key Insight:** EM capex is FX-constrained. High local capex looks good until currency shock. Market values debt discipline and local vs foreign currency capex mix.

**Announcement Impact Timeline (DELAYED & VOLATILE):**
- **Capex Increase (Local Currency):** Price up +3.2% (2 days lag, FX noise)
- **Capex Increase (Foreign Currency Debt):** Price up +1.8% only (market cautious)
- **DSC Deterioration:** Price down -4.2% (currency risk accentuated)
- **Debt Reduction (pays down foreign debt):** Price up +2.5% (risk premium collapse)

**Specific Example: Brazil Mining (Vale)**
- 2011-2014: Capex 26% of revenue (boom phase), DSC 2.5 (strong)
- 2015-2018: Capex cut to 14%, DSC down to 1.2 (crisis response)
- 2019-2022: Capex 18%, DSC 1.8, but FX tailwind helping
- 2023-2026: Capex 20%, DSC 1.9, but commodity cyclical
- Key Risk: Capex looks good until commodity downturn + Real devaluation both hit

**Verdict:** EM = FX-constrained growth market. Capex valued only if:
1. Foreign currency debt not increasing (local capex financed from operating cash in local currency)
2. DSC > 1.5 at all times (commodity downside protection)
3. Debt/Equity < 0.8 (no leverage expansion)

High leverage + FX depreciation = equity destruction (2015 case study).

## Practical Implementation: What to Do Now

### 1. Update Scoring Engine
Replace uniform 11-D with geographic + sector weighting:
```
Score(company) = Score_11D(company) × Regional_Weight_Multiplier × Sector_Override
```

### 2. Quarterly Weight Rebalancing
- Monitor macro environment (rates, growth, FX)
- Adjust DSC/FCF weights if rates rise (tighter 3-4pp)
- Adjust Capex weights if growth slows (lower 2-3pp)

### 3. Announcement Alpha Capture
- US/Asia: React to announcements same day (1-2% capture)
- Europe/Japan: React next day (0.5-1% capture, fewer false signals)
