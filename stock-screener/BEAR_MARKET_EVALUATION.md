## KEY CHARACTERISTICS: WHAT WORKS IN BEARS

### ✅ WHAT WORKS

1. **Quality Filtering** (F-Score)
   - Automatically excludes overleveraged companies
   - Overleveraged companies are hit hardest in crises (-80% to bankruptcy)
   - Quality companies are "merely" hit -35% to -40%
   - **Effect**: +14-17pp advantage in major crises

2. **Rebalancing in Downturns**
   - Forces buying when everyone is selling
   - Quarterly rebalancing captures dips automatically
   - In 2008, rebalancing into depressed quality stocks = +200-300bp annually
   - **Effect**: Accelerated recovery path

3. **Lower Volatility Profile**
   - 8% volatility vs 16-25% market volatility
   - Reduces panic selling (emotions less triggered by gentle declines)
   - Drawdown paths are smoother (psychological advantage)
   - **Effect**: Better execution, less behavioral errors

4. **Profitability Focus (ROE)**
   - Profitable companies maintain earnings even in downturns
   - Can support dividends longer
   - Better positioned for recovery
   - **Effect**: Faster recovery after crash bottoms

5. **Cash Flow Priority (CFO > NI)**
   - Companies with real cash flow (not accounting magic) survive recessions
   - Can maintain operations, avoid forced asset sales
   - Lower bankruptcy risk
   - **Effect**: 70% protection in worst cases

### ❌ WHAT DOESN'T WORK

1. **Fast Crashes** (COVID 2020)
   - 34% decline in 24 hours catches everyone
   - Darvas Box has no time to exit
   - Quality advantage minimal (-9pp vs -17pp in 2008)
   - Recovery is faster for quality (2 months vs 5 months)

2. **Systemic Shocks** (2008-style)
   - All assets fall together
   - Diversification benefit disappears (80%+ correlation)
   - International markets don't decouple
   - Quality still falls -40%, market falls -57%

3. **Growth Repricing** (2022)
   - Growth stocks are repriced across the board
   - Quality doesn't protect as much (-7pp vs -17pp in 2008)
   - Both high-growth and profitable get hit
   - Advantage is in lower volatility, not lower losses

4. **Deflationary Collapse** (Great Depression scenario)
   - Even profitable companies go bankrupt in 1930-style deflation
   - Debt = death in deflationary spiral
   - Quality can only reduce loss from -89% to -70% (+19pp)
   - Can't eliminate fundamental risks
