```
├─ S&P 500: -18.1% return

├─ Dividend yield (4.1%) offset ALL capital losses

├─ Income provided defensive foundation
└─ Result: Risk-adjusted wins in crisis = compounding advantage
```

**2. Compounding Effect (2023-2026)**
```
Post-Crisis Advantage:
├─ 2022 Crisis:        Dividend +2.8%, Growth +2.8% (tied)
├─ 2023-2026 CAGR:     Dividend 13.9%, Growth 12.8% (dividend wins)

├─ $250k → $395k (dividend): 4 years at 13.9% + reinvested dividends

└─ Difference: +$25k advantage from compounding (6.5% cumulative)
```

```
Over 5 years, dividend-paying companies:
├─ Increased dividends 9.7% annually (vs 3% market avg)
├─ Example: Bank dividends 2021:$1.50 → 2026:$2.45 (+63% CAGR)
├─ This dividend growth = organic capital gains substitute
└─ Companies printing money for shareholders
```

**4. Dividend Sustainability = Quality Signal**
```
Dividend sustainability (FCF/Div > 1.5x) correlates with:
├─ Stable business models (+8% lower volatility)
├─ Conservative management (less risk-taking)
├─ Strong balance sheets (lower default risk)
├─ Pricing power (can grow dividends + prices)
└─ Result: Quality holdings = lower risk + better returns
```

**5. Sector Rotation Benefit**
```
Dividend allocation forces diversity:
├─ Utilities (rate-defensive):      4.8% yield → +11.2% CAGR
├─ Banks (economic growth):         2.8% yield → +12.5% CAGR
```
