## REBALANCING & HOLDING PERIODS

### Quarterly Rebalancing (4x per year)

```
Portfolio Rebalancing Schedule:
├─ Q1 (Jan 1): Rebalance all positions to target weights
├─ Q2 (April 1): Rotate 50% of portfolio (highest conviction → lower conviction)
├─ Q3 (July 1): Rotate another 50% (bring in new quality picks)
└─ Q4 (Oct 1): Final rebalancing, set for year-end

Turnover Calculation:
├─ Annual turnover: 50% × 4 quarters = 2x annual turnover
├─ Average position age: 1.5 months (held for 1-2 rebalancing periods)
├─ Holding period range: 3-6 months (some positions held longer)
└─ Annual transaction cost: 1.5% (0.375% per rebalancing)

Position Evolution Example (Apple/AAPL):
├─ Jan 1: Buy AAPL at $150 (9% weight)
├─ March 31: Darvas signal breaks, downweight from 9% → 4.5% (sell half)
├─ April 1: Rebalance, new quality pick replaces sold half
├─ June 30: AAPL momentum revived, upgrade to 7.5% weight
├─ Oct 1: Year-end rebalance, trim to 9% if at 10%+ due to appreciation
└─ Jan 1 (Year 2): Original 9% weight, close the loop
```

### Average Holding Analysis

```
TURNOVER-BASED CALCULATION:
50% portfolio turnover quarterly means:
- 50% of portfolio is 3-month old (just bought)
- 50% of portfolio is 6+ months old (held from prior quarter)
- Weighted average: (50% × 3mo + 50% × 6mo) / 2 = 4.5 months

POSITION-SPECIFIC:
- Highest conviction (F-Score 8, strong Darvas): 6-12 months
- Medium conviction (F-Score 7, confirmed): 3-6 months
- Lower conviction (new entries): 1-3 months

RESULT:
- Average position age: 4-5 months
- Range: 1-12 months
- 50% of positions are younger than 3 months
- 25% of positions are older than 6 months
```
