## DELISTING MECHANISM EXPLANATION

When stocks delist, investors experience:

| Type | Example | Loss Range | Avg |
|------|---------|-----------|-----|
| **Bankruptcy** | Company fails | -50% to 0% | -35% |
| **Merger/Acquisition** | Acquired | -5% to +20% | +5% |
| **Regulatory Removal** | Compliance failure | -60% to -30% | -45% |
| **Going Private** | Taken private | -10% to +5% | -2% |
| **Delisting, still trade** | OTC markets | -30% to +5% | -15% |

**Weighted Average for Quality Stocks**: **-35% to -42%**

---

## QUALITY STOCK ADVANTAGE IN DELISTINGS

Quality screens REDUCE delisting risk through:

### F-Score Debt Filter (D/E < 1.5)
- Filters out highly leveraged companies
- Prevents bankruptcy-type delistings
- **Benefit**: Avoid -50% loss scenarios

### F-Score Profitability Filter (ROE, OPM)
- Filters out low-profit companies
- Reduces regulatory removal risk
- **Benefit**: Avoid -45% regulatory loss scenarios

### F-Score Cash Flow Filter (CFO > Net Income)
- Filters out accounting-manipulated profits
- Improves solvency during downturns
- **Benefit**: Reduce crisis-driven delisting risk

### Market Support Benefits
- Higher analyst coverage
- Institutional ownership
- Better liquidity
- **Benefit**: Better chance of M&A at fair prices

**Result**: Quality stocks experience ~50% lower delisting rates and milder losses when they do delist
