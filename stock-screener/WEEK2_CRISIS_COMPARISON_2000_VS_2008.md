## PART 1: CRISIS CHARACTERISTICS COMPARISON

### 2000 Dot-Com Crash: Sector Bubble

```
DEFINING CHARACTERISTICS:
├─ Root cause: Technology valuation bubble (P/E >100 for unprofitable firms)
├─ Sector affected: Primarily NASDAQ/tech stocks, other sectors held up
├─ Severity: NASDAQ -77%, but S&P -40%, Utilities -22%
├─ Duration: PROLONGED - 32 months from entry to near-bottom
├─ Trigger: Invesco drops IPO allocation limits (March 2000)
├─ Cascades: Amazon -95%, Yahoo -98%, Cisco -78%, Oracle -70%
├─ Safe havens: Utilities +2%, Staples -18%, Pharma -20%
└─ Recovery: Slow U-shaped 2002-2004 (tech took 5+ years to recover)

LESSONS FOR STRATEGY:
├─ Avoid sector entirely (don't buy cheap falling knives)
├─ Maximize defensive sectors (staples, pharma, utilities)
├─ Dividend yield matters (4%+ helps in long decline)
├─ Tax harvesting valuable (32-month decline = huge losses)
└─ Patience required (single sector may never recover fully)
```

### 2008 Financial Crisis: Systemic Collapse

```
DEFINING CHARACTERISTICS:
├─ Root cause: Subprime mortgage collapse, financial system breakdown
├─ Sector affected: ALL sectors hit, but quality outperforms
├─ Severity: S&P -57%, NASDAQ -56%, everything down together
├─ Duration: ACUTE - 9 months decline, then fast recovery
├─ Trigger: Lehman Brothers collapse (September 15, 2008)
├─ Cascades: Banks -90%, but also energy -50%, tech -40%
├─ Safe havens: Utilities -40%, Staples -32%, Pharma -20%
└─ Recovery: Fast V-shaped 2009-2010 (everything recovered 100%+ by 2010)

LESSONS FOR STRATEGY:
├─ Buy quality across sectors (recovery is broad-based)
├─ Sector diversification works (no single sector safe)
├─ Dividend yield minor (recovery comes from capital gains)
├─ Tax harvesting limited (quick recovery means small tax losses)
└─ Agility required (rotate back in fast once recovery starts)
```
